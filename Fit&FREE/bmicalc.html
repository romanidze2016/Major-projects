What is a BMI?

A BMI or a 'Body Mass Index' is a number that is calculated based on an individual's mass and height. As somebody gets taller, they will naturally weigh more, thus weight alone is not a sufficient measure for an individual's health status. However, it must be noted that an individual's BMI should only be used as a rough guide, as other factors such as body fat percentages, muscle mass can cause an individual to be misplaced.

Here are some rough guidelines as suggested by the World Health Organisation regarding BMI categories.

  • Less than 18.5 - Underweight
  • Between 18.5 and 25 - Normal
  • Between 25 and 30 - Overweight
  • Greater than 30 - Obese
